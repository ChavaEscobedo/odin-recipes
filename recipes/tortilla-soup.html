Tortilla Soup

Description

Tortilla Soup, also called Aztec Soup is a traditional Mexican Soup. The Tortilla Soup is made with ground tomato broth, garlic and onion, seasoned with epazote and served with fried strips of corn tortilla and pasilla chilli, avocado, asadero or fresh cheese and cream.

Its origin is associated ith the state of Tlaxcala in the center of the country. However, it has spread to all regions, both among the popular and wealthy classes.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 red peppers, chopped
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 4 cups vegetable stock
  • 1 can (14 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels, fresh or frozen
  • 1 cup Que Pasa Salsa - Medium
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground pepper
  • 1 avocado, peeled, pitted and chopped
  • 2 cups broken Que Pasa Yellow Corn Tortilla Chips
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. In large saucepan or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat; cook onion, garlic and red peppers, stirring occasionally, for about 5 minutes or until vegetables are softened.
  2. Add chili powder and cumin; cook, stirring, for 1 minute.
  3. Add stock, black beans and corn to saucepan; bring to boil.
  4. Reduce heat; cover and simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. Stir in salsa; season with salt and pepper.
  6. Divide among bowls and garnish with avocado, tortilla chips and cilantro.
