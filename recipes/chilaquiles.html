Chilaquiles

Description

Chilaquiles are basically corn tortilla pieces that are fried, cooked in salsa, and sprinkled with cheese. They are often served for breakfast with eggs and a side of beans or nopalitos.

Ingredients

  • 2 corn tortillas, preferably stale, or left out overnight to dry out a bit, quartered or cut into 6 wedges
  • Corn oil, or other neutral oil for frying
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 to 2 cups red chile sauce or salsa verde
  • Sprigs of epazote, optional

Steps

  1. Fry the tortillas:
    1. In a large saut√© pan, coat pan generously with corn oil, (1/8 inch), heat on medium high to high. When the oil is quite hot, add the tortillas, fry until golden brown.
    2. Remove tortillas to a paper towel lined plate to soak up excess oil. Sprinkle a little salt on the tortillas.
    3. Wipe pan clean of any browned bits of tortillas.
  2. Heat the salsa:
    1. Add 2 tablespoons of oil to pan, bring to high heat again.
    2. Add the salsa and let salsa cook for several minutes.
    3. If you have a few sprigs of epazote, add them to the salsa.
  3. Add fried tortillas to the salsa:
    1. Add the fried tortilla quarters to the salsa. Gently turn over the pieces of tortilla until they are all well coated with salsa. Let cook for a few minutes more.
    2. Remove from heat. Serve chilaquiles with garnishes and fried eggs and beans or nopalitos.
